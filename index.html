Beads of bondage 


Beads have been an important part of the Samburu community and continue to be very important till date.This is because beads signify beauty and they are also used to mark traditional and significant ceremonies.However beads in the Samburu community have turned to become beads of bondage through the tradition of girl child beading.Get to learn more about the the hidden vice in Samburu community.

What is girl child beading?

-This is a tradition in the Samburu community where a moran gives beads to an uncircumcised girl from the same clan as a sign of the commencement of a sexual relationship.
-Before the commencement of the relationship, the moran approaches the girl's mother and brothers and if they agree then the moran buys beads for the girl.
-The girl is given white and black beads to show that she is taken and nobody else can engage in a sexual relationship with her.
-When the relationship commences ,the girl’s mother builds a hut called a singira where the girl and the moran can have a sexual relationship.
-Despite the fact that contraceptives are not used during sex ,the girl is not supposed to get pregnant and if she happens to get pregnant ,the foetus must be aborted and if it is born the baby is killed through food poisoning or is thrown away.
-Sometimes the babies are adopted by the neighbouring communities.
The tradition of beading is not well known outside the samburu community as it is not discussed outside and it is a hidden practice within the community.
The practice of girl child beading has persisted in the community due to the lack of education and the fact that the culture is deeply embedded in the community.
Beading is a widely accepted rite of passage in the Samburu community and even the elders recommend it.
This practice started in 1780 and has been there since then.

          

How did beading come about? 

  • The origin of beading is traced back to sometimes in 1780s
  • Beading was recommended as a way of taming the warriors (morans) from admiring or stealing the elders' wives.
  • It started when the Samburu started separating age sets.
  • Beading came about after an incident where the warriors fought elders trying to take away their wives during a ceremony.
  • After the fight, there was a cleansing ceremony and it is during this ceremony that the idea of beading came about.
  • It was then agreed that the morans would be allowed to take uncircumcised girls as temporary brides through beading.
  • The warriors were forbidden from interacting with the elders and sleeping with their wives.
